1. Work through the assignment checklist before submitting your Bio project:
    • Does your document make a good first impression? Does it look polished and professional?
    • Does the document include a well-chosen photo that follows the #TuesdayTutorial: Taking a Professional Photo?
    • Does your document take advantage of the CRAP design principles?
      • Does the document use contrast to make key text (like the title) stand out?
      • Does the document use repetition so that the document is consistent and unified?
      • Does the document use alignment to connect the different sections and ensure nothing seems out of place?
      • Does the document use proximity to emphasize organization by keeping related information together?
      • Does the document use the CRAP principles to make the image contribute positively to the message? See #WeekendWatch: Adding Images to Your Text.
    • Does the document use strong details that present yourself in ways that will persuade your readers something about you? See #FridayFact: The Best Bio Isn’t About You.
    • Does the document use chunking to break the information into easy-to-read portions? Does it avoid presenting a "wall of text" by "chunking" text and visual elements.
    • Does the document arrange the information in a way that takes advantage of the F-shaped reading pattern? See #KnowYourReader: F-Shaped Reading Pattern.
    • Does the document use accurate/appropriate grammar, spelling, punctuation, mechanics, linking, and formatting? For a refresher on grammar and punctuation basics, watch the Lynda.com courses on Grammar Fundamentals and Advanced Grammar.

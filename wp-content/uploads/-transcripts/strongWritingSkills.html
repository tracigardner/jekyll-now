SURPRISE! People with Stronger Writing Skills are Better at Their Jobs, Get Paid More

This infographic explains the impact that good writing skills can have on your salary. The text on image shows yellow note book paper as the background and includes an image of pencil and an A+ circled in red.

Grammarly reviewed 448 freelance professionals’ profiles in eight categories (see footnote) on Elance, an online staffing platform. The work of these professionals was rated by employers (four or five stars). We then reviewed each profile for spelling, grammar, and punctuation errors. Our goal was to determine whether accurate or inaccurate writing in a freelancer’s personal profile is related to that person’s credibility, hireability, or pay. *Elance categories include: writing & translation, admin support, legal, design & multimedia, engineering & manufacturing, sales & marketing, finance & management, and IT & programming.

RESULTS:

The best-reviewed freelancers across all categories are those with the fewest writing errors per 100 words:

TAKEAWAY:

There are many possible explanations for the correlations we observed in this study. For example, people with stronger writing skills may simply be more attentive to details and thus better at their jobs. So, while Grammarly cannot imply causation between writing well and job performance/salary, we can say that professionals are judged every day by the quality of their writing. It never hurts to put your best food forward, grammatically speaking!
